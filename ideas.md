## Idea 1: American Craftsman — Rugged Patriot Workshop

**Design Movement**: Industrial Americana / Heavy Metal Print
**Core Principles**:
1. Unapologetic boldness — every element commands attention
2. Texture over flatness — grain, rust, aged steel overlays
3. Patriotic palette with raw material warmth
4. Asymmetric diagonal cuts between sections

**Color Philosophy**: Near-black (#0D0D0D) base with warm amber (#C8860A) as primary accent, pure white (#FFFFFF) text, and a single red stripe (#CC2222) echoing the logo's American flag stripe. The amber evokes aged wood and craftsmanship; the red signals urgency and pride.

**Layout Paradigm**: Full-bleed hero with diagonal clip-path transition into content. Service cards in a staggered 3-column grid with left-offset headers. Contact section uses a split-panel with a dark form on the left and a full-height construction photo on the right.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Diagonal steel-cut dividers between sections (clip-path polygon)
2. Bold condensed uppercase headings with a subtle letter-spacing
3. Red horizontal rule accents beneath section titles (echoing logo stripes)

**Interaction Philosophy**: Heavy, deliberate hover states — cards lift with a strong box-shadow and slight scale. CTA buttons use a fill-sweep animation from left to right on hover.

**Animation**: Entrance animations use a "forge" metaphor — elements slide in from the left as if being pushed into frame. Stagger delay on service cards. Hero text uses a fade-up with a slight blur-to-sharp transition.

**Typography System**:
- Display: "Oswald" (Bold 700) — condensed, industrial, matches logo energy
- Body: "Source Serif 4" (Regular 400) — warm, readable, craftsman authority
- Accent labels: "Oswald" (Regular 400) uppercase with wide letter-spacing

## Idea 2: New England Timber Frame — Dark Craftsman Elegance

**Design Movement**: Dark Arts & Crafts / New England Vernacular
**Core Principles**:
1. Deep charcoal backgrounds that let photography breathe
2. Warm wood-tone accents drawn directly from project photos
3. Serif typography for gravitas and permanence
4. Horizontal band layout with full-bleed photo sections

**Color Philosophy**: Deep charcoal (#1A1A18) background with warm wood amber (#B87333 — copper/bronze) as primary accent. Off-white (#F5F0E8) for body text. Red (#B22222) used sparingly for the "Call Mikey" CTA only, matching the logo. This palette mirrors the natural wood tones in the cover photo.

**Layout Paradigm**: Vertical stacking of full-width bands alternating between dark content sections and full-bleed photography. Service cards use a horizontal scroll on mobile, 3-column grid on desktop with left-aligned text and a top color bar.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Full-bleed construction photography with dark overlay gradients
2. Copper/bronze rule lines as section separators
3. Logo prominently displayed in the sticky nav bar

**Interaction Philosophy**: Smooth, confident transitions. Service cards reveal a description on hover with a bottom-to-top slide. The contact form fields use a bottom-border-only style (no box) for a refined look.

**Animation**: Scroll-triggered fade-in with slight upward translation. Hero text stagger: tagline first, then headline, then CTA. Parallax on hero background image.

**Typography System**:
- Display: "Playfair Display" (Black 900) — authoritative, timeless
- Body: "Lato" (Regular 400 / SemiBold 600) — clean, legible
- Accent: "Oswald" (Medium 500) for service card titles

## Idea 3: Steel & Sawdust — Military Blueprint Meets Workshop

**Design Movement**: Technical Blueprint / Workshop Manual
**Core Principles**:
1. Dark navy/charcoal base evoking technical drawings and blueprints
2. High-contrast white and yellow accent for visibility and urgency
3. Grid-ruled texture overlays suggesting precision and engineering
4. Off-axis layout with content blocks at slight angles

**Color Philosophy**: Dark navy (#0F1923) as the dominant background, bright construction yellow (#F5C518) as the primary accent (echoing caution tape and construction equipment), white (#FFFFFF) for text, and red (#CC2222) for the single most important CTA. This palette reads as "serious professional builder" rather than generic contractor.

**Layout Paradigm**: Blueprint-inspired with subtle grid lines in the background. Hero uses a large angled text block overlapping the construction photo. Service cards are styled as "spec sheets" with a header bar, icon, and bulleted specs. Social proof uses a horizontal ticker/marquee of stats.

**Signature Elements**:
1. Subtle blueprint grid texture overlay on dark sections
2. Yellow caution-stripe diagonal accents on section borders
3. "Spec sheet" service cards with a technical document aesthetic

**Interaction Philosophy**: Precise, mechanical interactions. Buttons have a sharp corner with a border that fills on hover. Cards flip or expand to reveal details. Cursor changes to crosshair on interactive elements.

**Animation**: Technical feel — elements "draw in" from edges like being drafted. Numbers in stats section count up when scrolled into view. Hero headline types in character by character.

**Typography System**:
- Display: "Bebas Neue" (Regular) — ultra-condensed, industrial power
- Body: "Roboto Condensed" (Regular 400) — technical, precise
- Accent: "Share Tech Mono" for stats and spec numbers

## Selected Design: Idea 1 — American Craftsman / Rugged Patriot Workshop

**Rationale**: This approach most faithfully mirrors the MC Sawyer brand identity — the logo's bold black/white/red/blue palette, the military-industrial energy, and the "forged in turmoil" founding story. The diagonal steel-cut dividers and Oswald condensed font directly echo the logo's visual language. The amber accent adds warmth from the construction photography without departing from the brand's core character.
